Optimal Stablecoin Contracts under Limited Commitment

Interactive simulation — Wesley Tan, Wesleyan University 2026 · Advisor: Prof. Ryuichiro Izumi

Model (§1–6). Contract = (σ, x). Two regimes: Regime I (σ < σ̄, fire sales); Regime II (σ ≥ σ̄, patient stayers block). σ̄ = (1−β−η)r/[β(R−r)], T* = (1−β−η)/β. M locks in before x is chosen (timing: σ announced → M forms → x optimised). Government forces full accommodation iff r > δG = R/(2−β), replicating Regime I.
Key welfare result (Prop. 4): At B-interior: WP−WG = (wB−wG)(wB+wG−rb)/[2Δ] > 0 whenever (BW) holds — sign equals sign(BW), so private always dominates at B-interior. WG > WP arises only in B-constrained (BW holds, σ̂B < σ̄): the forced carry rate σ̄ compresses MP via congestion cost gII = T*x²/2.

Comparative statics: σ* (carry rates) & wB−wG (welfare gap)

σI* = ½ − A/(2CI)  |  σII* = max(σ̄, σ̂B)  |  wG = 1+η/2+CI  |  wB = 1+η/2+CII/2

↑ param ∂σI* ∂σII* ∂(wB−wG) Key mechanism
Sign of wB−wG = CII/2 − CI: positive iff (BW) holds. At B-interior: WP−WG = (wB−wG)(wB+wG−rb)/[2Δ], so sign(WP−WG) = sign(BW) always.
Counterintuitive: ∂(wB−wG)/∂r < 0 — higher fire-sale recovery raises wG while leaving wB unchanged, shrinking private advantage. The B-interior green region is larger at lower r (but low r may force B-constrained → red).
