Optimal Stablecoin Contracts under Limited Commitment

Interactive simulation — Wesley Tan, Wesleyan University 2026 · Advisor: Prof. Ryuichiro Izumi

Model (§6–7). Contract = (σ, x). Regime I (σ < σ̄): fire sales absorb congestion. Regime II (σ ≥ σ̄ = (1−β−η)r/[β(R−r)]): patient stayers block, paying T* = (1−β−η)/β per deferred unit. Government replicates Regime I at (x̃, σ̂A). Aggregate welfare W = M · E[Ω], where market size M = (A + σ·f − g)/Δ, A = 1+η/2−rb.
WG > WP occurs only in the B-constrained regime (BW holds, σ̂B < σ̄): the required carry rate σ̄ is high, compressing MP via the congestion cost gII = T*x²/2. Government dominance requires large A (low rb), moderate β (high T*), and moderate R.

Comparative statics: optimal carry rates σ*   §6 Prop. 2–3

σI* = σ̂A = ½ − A/(2CI)  |  σII* = max(σ̄, σ̂B) where σ̂B = ¾ − AT*/(2(R−1)²)

Parameter ↑ ∂σI*/∂(·) ∂σII*/∂(·) Monotone? Mechanism
† η is the only non-monotone case for σII*. Sign of ∂σII*/∂η = sign(1+β+2η−2rb). At USDC calibration (rb=0.99, β=0.95, η=0.01): 1+0.95+0.02 = 1.97 < 1.98 = 2rb, so σII* decreases in η. For low rb the sign flips.

Key for WG > WP: Low β raises T* and σ̄ simultaneously. If σ̂B < σ̄ (B-constrained), the issuer is forced to carry at σ̄, compressing market size MP = (A + σ̄·fII − gII)/Δ below MG = (A + σA*·CI)/Δ when A is large (low rb).
